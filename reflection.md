# Reflection Report — Internship Application Campaign

**Giri Vignesh | CS Junior, Math Minor | Washington State University**

---

## Activity Description

I am currently in the midst of conducting an extensive internship job search for an internship for a summer 2025 Software Engineering or Project Management position. To this end, I applied for a total of about 350 internships between both positions, I created and tailored resumes to conform to each of these internship positions, restructured my LinkedIn profile, and participated in several WSU-sponsored networking activities. When I started this process, I had no definitive direction in mind regarding whether I wanted to pursue an internship in Software Engineering (SWE) or an internship in Project Management (PM). My internship job search therefore began quite broadly, and I was applying for both positions while trying to narrow my options down in the end.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

My key to changing my job hunt was **creating a separated strategy** in the middle of it all. Initially, I applied to too broad a market for SWE and PM roles without differentiating how I would present myself for either. Ultimately, I have realized that this is the reason behind my low number of applications. My resume having to communicate too many unrelated messages to two very different audiences at the same time dilutes the volume of applicants to the position I want most at the moment (but still want overall). Therefore, I created two separate resumes for SWE and PM that detail my SWE projects and class work versus my experience with coordination, communication, and cross-functional collaboration and how I have performed well using those skills.

My attitude about networking changed through my experiences at networking events as well. Speaking with recruiters and alumni has given me the best understanding of what hiring teams are truly looking for and also is pushing me to become more deliberate with the companies I am spending extra time on versus being able just to apply to every job you see on the job boards.

## Contributions

All of this was completely self-guided work — all elements, including creating the applications, revising resumes, and updating LinkedIn profiles are created without guidance. There is a small amount of collaboration through the networking events, but only because talking to other students or professionals allows me to evaluate if my approach was correct or if I needed to adjust my expectations about the job market.

## Quality Assessment

After applying to nearly three hundred and fifty different jobs, with only two interviews to show for my efforts, it is clear that I could have done better with this experience. On the one hand, I submitted too many applications too early in the process (without knowing specifically which jobs would be my best bets) and therefore did not take the time to consider how qualified I was for any particular position. Also, I submitted too many generic, uncustomized applications instead of putting more effort into individually researching and customizing those applications.

In retrospect, I realize that I could have had more success by spending more time applying to fewer jobs (100 high-quality applications) that were better targeted to me than submitting too many lower-quality applications (350 generic applications).

Additionally, I will start networking earlier and developing professional materials (resume, cover letter, etc) ahead of time so that they are ready for distribution when recruiting season begins.

## Career Alignment

Even though an offer wasn't made, my personal development has been greatly improved by this experience. I've got a very solid understanding of my competitive landscape, what skills I need to work on, and how the hiring process for PM and SWE is different from one another. I feel like I am so much in a better place going into my senior year than I was. I know which resume to send to which company, I have contacts from the networking events I went to now I can follow up with them, and I now have a much better understanding of the time/range of effort required to find a job that I actually want to have. I'm now changing the focus to go into a full-time PM or SWE position after graduation, and I still have internship opportunities to help connect me to a job. This first step of my job search is really just the start of the long journey to that goal.
