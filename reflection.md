# Reflection Report — Internship Application Campaign

**Giri Vignesh | CS Junior, Math Minor | Washington State University**

---

## Activity Description

My career-aligned adventure was a full-scale internship job search conducted throughout the 2024–2025 academic year. The goal was straightforward: secure a summer 2025 internship in Software Engineering or Project Management. I submitted roughly 350 applications across both role types, developed tailored resumes for each track, refreshed my LinkedIn profile, and attended multiple WSU-hosted networking events. The scope was deliberately broad early on, reflecting uncertainty about which direction — SWE or PM — I wanted to commit to.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

The most consequential decision I made was **narrowing my targeting mid-cycle**. Early on I applied widely across both SWE and PM roles without strong differentiation in how I presented myself. Over time I realized this was diluting my applications — my resume was trying to speak to two very different audiences simultaneously. I began maintaining two clearly distinct resume versions: one that led with technical projects and coursework for SWE roles, and one that emphasized coordination, communication, and cross-functional thinking for PM roles.

Attending networking events also shaped my strategy. Speaking directly with recruiters and alumni helped me understand what hiring teams were actually looking for, and pushed me to be more intentional about which companies I invested extra effort in rather than spray-and-pray across every job board listing.

## Contributions

This was entirely independent work. Every application, resume revision, and LinkedIn update was self-directed. The networking events were the closest thing to a collaborative element — conversations with other students and professionals helped me pressure-test my approach and recalibrate expectations about the market.

## Quality Assessment

Submitting ~350 applications and receiving only two interviews is a humbling outcome, and I think it reflects a few things I would do differently. I applied too broadly too early without a clear enough sense of which roles I was a strongest candidate for. I also underinvested in the quality of individual applications at the start, prioritizing volume over targeting. In hindsight, spending more time on 100 well-researched, personalized applications would likely have outperformed 350 generic ones.

I would also start earlier — both in building relationships through networking and in having polished materials ready before peak recruiting season hits.

## Career Alignment

Despite the lack of an offer, this experience meaningfully advanced my professional development. I now have a clearer picture of where I sit competitively, what skills I need to develop, and how PM and SWE hiring processes differ from each other. Going into my senior year, I am better positioned than I was: I know which resume to send where, I have contacts from networking events I can follow up with, and I have a more realistic sense of the timeline and effort required to land a role I actually want. The goal shifts now to full-time PM or SWE positions post-graduation, with internship opportunities still on the table as a bridge. This search was step one of that longer journey.
